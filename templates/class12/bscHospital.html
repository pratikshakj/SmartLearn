Bachelor of Science in Hospitality Studies is a 3-year full time graduation course, the eligibility for which is a Higher Secondary Certificate (10+2) qualification with a minimum of 50% marks in any stream. The program is spread over 3 years, divided into 6 semesters of six months each. Admission to the course in various recognized universities is based on entrance tests held followed by an interview, besides taking the 12th marks into consideration, to judge the eligibility of the candidate seeking admission. The course is best suited for those who are seeking a career in the hospitality and services sector. A hospitality studies student will be taught the process of dealing in reception and treatment of guests. The program offers vast opportunities in the travel and tourism sector, hospitality and services sector, and general management as it offers a comprehensive study focused on the management of food, beverage, and accommodation of guests. A degree in hospitality studies will allow you to deal with such matters from both a technical and operative context. Such graduates may be hired as Hospitality Executive, Catering Officer, Manager& Supervisor, Cabin Crew Hostess/Host, Field Supervisor, Marketing Manager, Catering Supervisor & Assistant, Manager and/or Supervisor, etc. They may be hired in fields such as the Tourism Industry, Hotels/Restaurants, Airlines, Cruise Ships, Tourism Industry, Retail Industry, Colleges/Universities, etc. The average full time fee charged for the course in India ranges between INR 60,000 to INR 5.10 lacs, and the average salary offered to such professionals’ ranges between INR 2 lacs to 15 lacs per annum, increasing with experience and expertise.

B.Sc. in Hospitality Studies: What is it about?

This hospitality management degree has been designed to offer to eligible students the knowledge of management of food, beverage, and accommodation along with general management. It will help the candidates to get the technical and operational perspectives required for functioning in the hospitality and services sector. The program encompasses marketing, financial management, human resource management besides also focussing on honing the communication skills of the candidates. The course also provides a comprehensive and in-depth laboratory work for students to gain the knowledge and the required skill standards for functioning of the core departments of any hotel related to food and beverage production and management, catering operations, general management, human resource management, finance management, tourism marketing, among others. The said course, in its final semesters, will require the candidates to demonstrate their computer skills to address management challenges in the fields of marketing, cost control, and human resource management, all of which are included in the course syllabus; guest service skills; apply tools to monitor the management and functioning of day-to-day services of lodging and/or food service business. The candidate must also, by then, possess, proper sanitation, food safety procedures and methods, appropriate use of common kitchen and dining room management.

Eligibility for B.Sc. in Hospitality Studies

Candidates wishing to apply for the course need to have passed their Higher Secondary Certificate with a minimum of 50% marks in any stream, from a recognized board. After application, some colleges/institutes may hold an entrance test followed by an interview to judge the eligibility of the candidate for the course. B.Sc. in Hospitality Studies: Who should Opt?

The program, with studies concentrating on marketing, human resources, and financial management, will equip the candidate to build a strong base before they seek for a professional career in the hospitality and services sector. The candidates who have analytical, critical ,and problem-solving skills along with computer skills to create and present data bases and spread sheets will benefit the most from this course. The person must also have the flexibility, will, and the qualities required to work with a team or lead a team. Hospitality majorly involves dealing with guests, and so, the person must possess an amiable personality, friendly yet professional way of approaching the guests, ability to solve problems rationally, and sharp leadership skills. Such graduates, trained and educated in accountancy, cost control, and management are adept to be managers and supervisors with their sharpened skills of communication and the ability to host and treat guests in an amiable manner. In the process of such training, the students also get the opportunity to sharpen their analytical, critical and problem-solving skills which will again help them sustain in the travel and tourism, hospitality and services sector.
